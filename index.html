Côte d'Ivoire

Côte d'Ivoire is a West African country with beach towns, tropical forests and a French colonial heritage. With a surface area of 322,462 km2, it is bordered to the north by Mali and Burkina Faso, to the west by Liberia and Guinea, to the east by Ghana and to the south by the Atlantic Ocean. The population was estimated at 22,671,331 in 2014. The political and administrative capitals of Côte d'Ivoire are Yamoussoukro and Abidjan.It is known for its rich cultural diversity, vibrant economy, and beautiful landscapes. Côte d'Ivoire is famous for its cocoa production, which makes it one of the largest exporters of chocolate in the world.
