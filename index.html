Yamoussoukro

The Basilique Notre-Dame de la Paix in Yamoussoukro, Côte d'Ivoire, is an emblematic monument built between 1985 and 1990 on the initiative of Félix Houphouët-Boigny, the country's first president.Its appearance is reminiscent of St. Peter's Basilica in Rome, and it is one of the largest churches in the world. The basilica's architecture is impressive, measuring some 158 meters long and 60 meters wide, with a dome reaching 60 meters in height. It can accommodate around 18,000 worshippers. As an important religious center, it is a place of pilgrimage for Catholics. The basilica is also a tourist attraction, drawing many visitors thanks to its majestic architecture and history. Surrounded by magnificent gardens, it offers a peaceful setting for meditation and prayer, making it a powerful symbol of culture and spirituality in Côte d'Ivoire.
